# Child Clarity Playtest

Use this checklist for one short supervised session per player. Record observations by profile only; do not store names, audio, video, or other identifying information in the repository.

## Setup

- Use a landscape tablet with sound enabled and a stable local build.
- Reset the selected profile's save before beginning.
- Run Grade 2 Mage and Grade 5 Ranger/Adventurer as separate sessions.
- Explain only: "Explore the farm and see what you can discover."
- Do not point at controls, read objectives, or suggest answers unless the player is genuinely stuck.

## 10-15 Minute Route

Observe whether the player can:

1. Choose their hero and enter the farm.
2. Discover movement and the ACTION button.
3. Notice the objective and find Mira.
4. Reach the crop patch and open its optional bonus.
5. Grade 2 only: discover and use READ ALOUD.
6. Skip or answer incorrectly and still recognize that the adventure continues.
7. Find and interact with the Practice Slime.
8. Return to Mira and notice the gold and Sunberry Charm reward.
9. Begin or complete the optional Whispering Scarecrow errand if time remains.
10. Reload the game and recognize that progress was saved.

## Observation Notes

For each moment of hesitation, record:

- Profile: Grade 2 or Grade 5.
- Step and approximate elapsed time.
- What the player tried before asking for help.
- Exact help required, if any.
- Whether the issue blocked progress, caused repeated confusion, or was cosmetic.

Do not interpret or redesign during the session. Capture what happened first.

## Acceptance Gate

The slice is ready for the Grade 5 production-actor milestone when both profiles:

- complete Mira's first errand without progression help;
- can leave every optional prompt by answering or skipping;
- understand that a wrong answer does not stop the quest;
- can identify the objective and ACTION control without repeated coaching; and
- retain quest progress and rewards after reload.

Fix only demonstrated blockers, unreadable controls, and repeated confusion before the next checkpoint. Send curriculum, story, economy, reward-balance, or broader kid-UX findings to ChatGPT for product review.
