## 5. Seam, repetition, palette, and motif audit

### Visual inspection

- **Comparison panel / 3×3 / field previews**: no hard grid lines, no cross-shaped or checkerboard artifact at any tile boundary; the mottled brown texture reads continuously across every internal seam.
- **No baked objects**: no flowers, rocks, footprints, borders, or decorative elements anywhere in the 16×16 result — pure mottled dirt texture.
- **No visible plus/cross motif**: this was the specific defect flagged in ChatGPT's runtime audit of the original AI source. Visual inspection of all four evidence images shows no plus- or cross-shaped high-contrast pattern at any scale.
- **Expected single-tile repetition**: because only `center` exists, the 3×3 and 12×8 previews show the *same* 16×16 tile repeating at a fixed period — expected, and exactly why `edge_*`/`corner_*`/`inner_corner_*` variants exist for later batches (out of scope here).

### Deterministic edge/wrap check

Computed directly from the normalized 16×16 RGBA pixel data (not eyeballed):

| Metric | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Average column-wrap step (col 15 → col 0) | 10.95 (RGB Euclidean distance) |
| Average internal column step | 10.62 |
| **Wrap ÷ internal ratio (horizontal)** | **1.031** |
| Average row-wrap step (row 15 → row 0) | 9.39 |
| Average internal row step | 11.73 |
| **Wrap ÷ internal ratio (vertical)** | **0.800** |

A seam would show up as the wrap step being *much larger* than the typical internal step (an abrupt jump not seen anywhere else in the texture, typically several times larger). The vertical ratio (0.800) is below 1, matching the pattern seen in `grass_a`'s audit. The horizontal ratio (1.031) is marginally above 1 — the wrap-boundary step is about 3% larger than the average internal step, which is well within normal texture-noise variation and not a seam signature (a real hard seam reads as a large multiple, not a few percent). Neither axis shows an outlier jump.

### Directional brightness gradient check

Per-row and per-column average luminance (Rec. 601-ish weights) was computed and correlated against row/column index:

- Row-luminance range: 4.09 (≈4.3% of the tile's mean luminance), correlation with row index: **-0.055**
- Column-luminance range: 5.89 (≈6.2% of mean), correlation with column index: **-0.104**

Both correlations are close to 0 (far from ±1), meaning the luminance variation across the tile is textural noise, not a monotonic lightening/darkening trend. No directional gradient that would expose repetition.

### Palette compatibility (`forest` + `wood_leather` families)

`farm_environment_palette_v1.json` locks `forest` to `["#0A3521", "#174F1D", "#325E19", "#427118", "#6C8B15", "#91A513"]` and `wood_leather` to `["#412E15", "#674B1F", "#926B2A", "#B98535", "#D5A342"]` — the two families this target's spec declares (`paletteFamilies: ["wood_leather", "forest"]`). For every one of the 256 pixels in the normalized tile, the nearest-swatch RGB Euclidean distance (across both families combined) was computed: **min 11.4, median 25.2, max 32.5 — all 256/256 pixels within 40 units of a locked swatch.** The candidate reads as natural shading/variation of the locked palette, not off-family color.

### Plus/cross motif scan (deterministic)

Directly targeting the defect ChatGPT's audit flagged in the original high-resolution source: for every interior pixel, checked whether all four axis-neighbors (N/S/E/W) differ from the center pixel by a large, similar delta (the signature of a hard cross/plus edge). **0 out of 196 interior pixels** matched this pattern. Combined with the visual inspection above, the normalized 16×16 runtime representation shows no trace of the flagged motif.

### Format checks

- No JPEG artifacts possible: master, canonical source, and normalized output are all PNG (lossless) end-to-end.
- No unexpected alpha: normalized output alpha channel is uniformly 255 (fully opaque) across all 256 pixels.
- Runtime smoothing: not applicable at this stage — no runtime integration occurred (out of scope for this task). The project-wide engine setting is nearest/point sampling (`VISUAL_ASSET_CONTRACT.md` §3); this was not independently re-verified here since nothing was loaded into Phaser.
